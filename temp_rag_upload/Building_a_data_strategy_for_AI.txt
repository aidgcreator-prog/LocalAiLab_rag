Ready
or not, the AI arms race
is in full swing.
Businesses around the globe
are battling to be the first
to realize the full
potential of advanced AI.
But what will
separate tomorrow's AI
leaders from the followers?
Data.
Welcome to AI Academy.
My name is Cathy Reese.
I'm a Senior Partner for Data 
and AI Technology Transformation
at IBM Consulting.
You've probably heard
the phrase: Without data,
there is no AI.
But the more important truth
is that without quality data,
you can't get quality AI.
Data is the fuel that
powers all AI capabilities
and use cases.
And while most organizations
have plenty of fuel in the tank,
they struggle to produce
advanced AI models that drive
significant business value.
Why?
Because their
data isn't ready.
On the road to
operationalized AI, harnessing
your highest-quality data
assets will be the ultimate
competitive differentiator.
Though more organizations are
adopting the AI technology of
the future, their data
strategies are often relics
of the past.
A data strategy is essential
to creating the high quality,
accessible, and secure data
needed to fuel enterprise AI.
With the rise of advanced
AI – generative and agentic AI –
organizations have to
modernize their data strategies.
Unstructured data is
the primary training ground
for advanced AI.
Around 80 to 90% of
enterprise data is unstructured.
And until now, it's
been an untapped source
of potential business value.
That's because raw datasets
are impossible to validate,
understand, and convert
without modern data governance –
a key component of an
AI-ready data strategy.
Now advanced AI adoption is
forcing everyone to reevaluate
the condition of
their data estate.
The result?
Very few companies
trust their own data.
In fact, only 29% of tech
leaders believe their data
has the necessary quality,
accessibility, and security
to scale advanced AI.
For everyone else,
persistent data quality issues
stand in the
way of adoption.
For example, unstructured data 
is often spread across disparate
systems and environments.
Most IT architectures
aren't intentionally designed
to manage large
amounts of raw data.
Whether in the cloud,
on prem, in applications,
or at the edge,
disconnected data sources
create silos, leading
to incomplete datasets,
duplication, and bias.
Data ownership has
also become more precarious.
The only way to create
accountability for how your
data is collected, used,
and processed is by clearly
defining data owners,
including external owners like
data vendors, otherwise the
risks of breaches, corruption,
and inconsistencies will loom 
large across your organization.
Speaking of data breaches, 
advanced AI presents new,
unpredictable security threats.
There are unique challenges
to data security that aren't
covered by policies and
procedures of years past.
Highly regulated sectors
like government or healthcare
are especially vulnerable
because of the sensitive
personal data they hold.
Implementing security
protocols that account
for changes in
technology are a must.
There's very
little room for error.
Ultimately, bad
data fuels bad AI.
When an AI model
seemingly goes rogue,
it's just amplifying
insights from the data
it's been trained on.
Any inaccuracies,
inconsistencies, or biases
present in your data will
also be present in your AI.
So, what steps can
you take to overcome
these data challenges?
How can you create
high-quality data that's
ready for advanced AI?
And how can you leverage
your data and AI to achieve
your business objectives?
Remember, it's all
about your data strategy.
In a recent survey, 93% of
chief data officers agreed
that a data strategy is
crucial for getting value
out of advanced AI.
Your data strategy is a
roadmap for effective data
collection, storage, and usage.
It builds trust in your data
foundation, enabling informed
decision-making and
more reliable outcomes.
To design a successful
data strategy, you'll want to
work backwards, starting
with a specific outcome.
Let's break that down.
Start with your ideal outcome
in mind to quickly determine if
you have the data you
need to actually implement
your use case.
This also ensures your data
initiatives always align with
your broader business goals.
Ask your stakeholders a few
key questions to get started.
Is there a specific
problem we want to solve?
Have you identified a
high ROI opportunity?
Are there measurable
metrics we can hit quickly?
Answer these questions
first to identify simple
use cases that
can net big returns.
After selecting a use
case, conduct an inventory
of your data estate.
This is an important
step because the success
of your advanced AI is
directly tied to the state
of your data landscape.
Assess the accuracy,
accessibility, and relevance
of your data.
You won't need to see or
ingest all your data at once.
Just ensure you have
the data you need for this
particular use case.
Gen AI itself can actually
streamline this process.
Large language models can
decipher unstructured data,
enabling global
data transformation.
We recently helped a
commercial client build gen AI
scripts that corrected data
inconsistencies, redundancies,
and errors before a
large-scale data migration.
The gen AI scripts used
machine learning to understand
the underlying source
and target data structures
then automated the
migration between each system.
The client was able to
complete their data migration
eight times faster using
gen AI than with traditional
tools and processes.
Once you have
the right assets,
review your data policies.
Policy is a key component of 
your data governance framework.
It's where you'll establish
the controls and processes that
ensure data quality –
your key competitive
differentiator for AI.
Revisit and adjust
the guidelines you've
set for data
collection, ownership, storage,
processing, and usage.
This isn't a one-person job.
Data teams will need
to collaborate to link
disparate datasets and
update access controls.
Next, you'll want to
evaluate your IT estate.
Unstructured data will
bring new file formats into
your data landscape, so you may
need to upgrade your tech stack.
Be intentional here.
Adopting hybrid-by-design
principles will help you build
an architecture
that supports your
interconnected business needs.
An open data lakehouse,
for example, allows you to
accommodate and access
multiple data formats for
your use case no
matter where they reside.
The solutions are out there.
It's just about
implementing the ones
that work best
for your business.
Finally, foster
a data-first culture.
Data leaders need to be
empowered to imbue a strong
data-driven culture on
every level of the business.
This is your chance to
embed a sense of personal
responsibility around data
security and privacy through
literacy initiatives,
data democratization, and
skill-building programs.
It's critical for all
business leaders to work
towards this goal together.
A CDO can't be successful
without support from the other
members of the C-suite.
Everyone, starting from
the top, must believe in the
value of data.
A successful data
strategy allows you to
effectively leverage
your data as an asset
to generate business value.
It's a roadmap
that guides you to
new market opportunities, 
operational efficiencies,
product improvements,
and competitive gains.
Most importantly, it provides
you with the high-quality data
that fuels the most powerful
AI capabilities and the most
successful use cases.
If you want to
compete in the AI arms race,
it's time to design
your data strategy.
